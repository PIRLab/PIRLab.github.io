Industry Application

Physical intelligence for real engineering systems.

PIRLab translates robot perception, prediction, mapping, and action into application cases for infrastructure, construction, mobility, and digital twins.

Application Stack

From 3D sensing to deployable automation.

The CV highlights a deployment-oriented agenda: robot world models for engineering automation, infrastructure automation, construction robotics, 3D vision, SLAM, and robot manipulation.

The cases below organize that work by industrial use: what the system perceives, what model it builds, and how that model supports action or decision making.

Case Highlights

Representative industrial application cases.

Engineering automation

Robotics for construction and infrastructure workflows

Robot world models support site-scale understanding, task reasoning, and automation loops for engineering environments that change over time.

Digital twins

Dense semantic maps for built assets

Neural and Gaussian mapping methods reconstruct actionable 3D scene representations for inspection, retrofitting, monitoring, and simulation.

Mobility

Autonomous driving perception and localization

Image-LiDAR registration, LiDAR odometry, segmentation, tracking, and scene flow create a robust perception layer for mobile platforms.

Manipulation

Real2Sim2Real robot learning

3D Gaussian Splatting and reinforcement learning connect real scenes, simulation, and physical manipulation for task-level robot action.

Construction robotics

Robotic brick stacking

Action reasoning in 3D space uses language and spatial models to support robot task planning for brick stacking and site automation.

Building retrofitting

HVAC and roof digital twins

Point cloud and image data are converted into geometric asset models for HVAC generation, roof retrofitting, and building information retrieval.

Road maintenance

Road-surface reconstruction

Multimodal road data, explicit meshes, and implicit encoding support large-scale road-surface mapping and maintenance-oriented analysis.

Infrastructure sensing

Low-cost LiDAR reconstruction

Sparse infrastructure point clouds can be restored and reconstructed with diffusion models, prompted segmentation, and dense neural mapping.

Energy infrastructure

Hydropower digital twins

Geometric modelling of hydropower plants creates a foundation for digital twin workflows in inspection, monitoring, and asset management.

Autonomous systems

Localization, tracking, and scene flow

2D-3D registration, multi-object tracking, LiDAR odometry, and 3D scene flow form a practical perception stack for dynamic environments.
