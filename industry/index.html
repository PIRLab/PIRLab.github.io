Industry Application

Physical intelligence for real engineering systems.

PIRLab organizes industrial application around three modules: autonomous mobile robots and autonomous driving, robot brains for embodied intelligence and VLA, and industrial autonomous robots for construction automation.

Application Stack

From 3D sensing to deployable automation.

The CV highlights a deployment-oriented agenda: robot world models for engineering automation, infrastructure automation, construction robotics, 3D vision, SLAM, and robot manipulation.

The modules below organize that work by industrial capability: how robots perceive and localize, how robot brains reason and act, and how autonomous robots operate in construction and infrastructure environments.

Three Modules

Industrial applications are grouped by deployable robotic capability.

Module 01

Autonomous Mobile Robots / Autonomous Driving

Localization, mapping, tracking, segmentation, odometry, and scene-flow perception for robots and vehicles operating in dynamic environments.

Module 02

Robot Brain / Embodied Intelligence / VLA

World models, action reasoning, VLA-style interfaces, and Real2Sim2Real learning that connect perception with physically grounded robot action.

Module 03

Industrial Autonomous Robots / Construction Automation

Construction robotics, infrastructure automation, digital twins, retrofitting, road maintenance, and industrial inspection workflows.

Module 01

Autonomous mobile robots and autonomous driving.

2D-3D localization

Image-LiDAR registration for vehicle localization

Image-LiDAR registration, LiDAR odometry, segmentation, tracking, and scene flow create a robust perception layer for mobile platforms.

LiDAR odometry

Large-scale 3D localization and motion estimation

Deep LiDAR odometry and point-cloud registration support reliable state estimation for mobile robots and autonomous driving systems.

Perception stack

Localization, tracking, and scene flow

2D-3D registration, multi-object tracking, LiDAR odometry, and 3D scene flow form a practical perception stack for dynamic environments.

Road autonomy

Road-surface reconstruction

Multimodal road data, explicit meshes, and implicit encoding support large-scale road-surface mapping and maintenance-oriented analysis.

Robust deployment

Dynamic scene understanding

Segmentation, occupancy refinement, and motion prediction help robots reason about moving objects and changing road environments.

Module 02

Robot brain, embodied intelligence, and VLA.

Real2Sim2Real

Robot learning with 3D scene representations

3D Gaussian Splatting and reinforcement learning connect real scenes, simulation, and physical manipulation for task-level robot action.

World models

Prediction as the robot brain's memory and imagination

Scene-flow prediction, neural mapping, and action-conditioned representations support embodied reasoning before robots move.

VLA

Vision-Language-Action interfaces

Language-guided action reasoning can translate task intent into physically meaningful robot decisions in structured 3D environments.

Embodied AI

Action reasoning in 3D space

Spatial reasoning and world models provide the middle layer between perception outputs and robot policies.

Robot learning

Simulation-to-real transfer

Real2Sim2Real pipelines let robot brains train, test, and adapt policies while staying grounded in real scene geometry.

Module 03

Industrial autonomous robots and construction automation.

Construction automation

Robotics for construction and infrastructure workflows

Robot world models support site-scale understanding, task reasoning, and automation loops for engineering environments that change over time.

Digital twins

Dense semantic maps for built assets

Neural and Gaussian mapping methods reconstruct actionable 3D scene representations for inspection, retrofitting, monitoring, and simulation.

Construction robotics

Robotic brick stacking

Action reasoning in 3D space uses language and spatial models to support robot task planning for brick stacking and site automation.

Building retrofitting

HVAC and roof digital twins

Point cloud and image data are converted into geometric asset models for HVAC generation, roof retrofitting, and building information retrieval.

Infrastructure sensing

Low-cost LiDAR reconstruction

Sparse infrastructure point clouds can be restored and reconstructed with diffusion models, prompted segmentation, and dense neural mapping.

Energy infrastructure

Hydropower digital twins

Geometric modelling of hydropower plants creates a foundation for digital twin workflows in inspection, monitoring, and asset management.
